ChipGlobe RTL2GDS Implementation, Layout, and Verification Services

Welcome to ChipGlobe's RTL2GDS services page, where innovation meets precision in chip development. At ChipGlobe, we are dedicated to delivering top-notch RTL2GDS implementation, layout, and verification services, using our extensive experience and cutting-edge technology to bring your concepts into high-quality silicon

Our Expertise in RTL2GDS Implementation

ChipGlobe specializes in the RTL2GDS (Register Transfer Level to GDSII) implementation, which covers the entire backend flow of chip design, from the digital logic design phase to physical layout. We ensure that our designs are optimized for power, performance, and area, aligning closely with our clients' goals.

  • Front-End to Back-End Flow: We handle every aspect of RTL2GDS implementation, including synthesis, place and route (PnR), clock tree synthesis (CTS), and sign-off checks. Our structured workflow guarantees the highest quality in each phase, from initial RTL code to a fully functional GDSII layout ready for manufacturing.
  • Synthesis and Layout: Using industry-standard tools like Cadence and Synopsys, our team focuses on optimizing chip layouts for efficient area usage, minimal power consumption, and maximum performance.
  • Timing Closure and DRC/LVS: Our experts ensure that all timing, power, and physical design constraints are satisfied through rigorous Static Timing Analysis (STA), Design Rule Checks (DRC), and Layout vs. Schematic (LVS) checks.
  • Power Integrity: We integrate IR drop analysis and electromigration checks to ensure power integrity across the design, particularly critical for high-speed and complex chips.

Functional Verification and Quality Assurance

Our functional verification process ensures that designs meet all functional requirements before moving to physical implementation. We employ advanced verification methodologies including UVM, metric-driven verification, and constraint random verification to achieve thorough test coverage.

  • Verification Environments: We set up verification environments from scratch, developing reusable testbenches and employing industry-standard tools such as Cadence and Mentor.
  • Low Power Verification: We utilize power-aware verification methods, including Unified Power Format (UPF), to ensure that all power domains are functioning as intended, thereby optimizing both performance and energy efficiency.
  • ISO 26262 Compliance: For automotive projects, we integrate safety verification processes in line with ISO 26262, ensuring functional safety from early development phases.

Tools and Technologies We Use

  • EDA Tools: Our services leverage the power of Cadence, Synopsys, and Mentor tools to ensure a robust backend design flow.
  • Languages and Methodologies: Our experts are proficient in SystemVerilog, VHDL, Verilog, and scripting languages like Python, TCL, and Perl for automation and tool customization.
  • Verification Methodologies: We employ industry-leading methodologies such as UVM (Universal Verification Methodology) and OVM (Open Verification Methodology) to ensure that all designs are exhaustively verified and ready for production.
